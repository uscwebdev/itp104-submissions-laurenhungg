People

Juggling the intricacies of college life, where academic pursuits, work obligations, and nurturing friendships intertwine, presents a multifaceted challenge. In this dynamic environment, Robert Frost's timeless poem, "A Time to Talk," resounds as a poignant call to recognize the enduring importance of maintaining connections with loved ones. The vast geographical separation of 6,846 miles and a 15-hour time difference between my mother and me adds an extra layer of complexity, making it challenging to find those precious moments for quality interactions. Yet, the poem inspires a thoughtful reconsideration of priorities, encouraging the intentional carving out of time for meaningful connections despite the constraints imposed by distance and busy schedules.
